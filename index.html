Important Cities

Zurich

Switzerland’s largest city, Zurich is a global financial hub. Known for its luxury shopping, Lake Zurich, museums, and vibrant nightlife.

Geneva

Home to the United Nations and the Red Cross, Geneva is famous for its watchmaking, Lake Geneva, and international diplomacy.

Bern

The capital of Switzerland, Bern’s Old Town is a UNESCO World Heritage site, with medieval arcades, fountains, and the famous Bear Park.

Lucerne

Surrounded by mountains, Lucerne is known for its Chapel Bridge, medieval old town, and beautiful lake views.

Interlaken

A famous adventure sports hub between two lakes, Interlaken is the gateway to the Jungfrau region of the Swiss Alps.

How to Move Around

Trains

The Swiss Federal Railways (SBB) is efficient, punctual, and scenic. Famous routes include the Glacier Express and Bernina Express.

Trams & Buses

Cities like Zurich, Basel, and Geneva have extensive tram networks. Buses connect rural areas and mountain villages.

Boats & Cars

Lake ferries are scenic ways to travel, especially on Lake Geneva and Lake Lucerne. Cars are useful in alpine villages not connected by trains.

Famous Swiss Food

Fondue

A classic dish of melted cheese served in a communal pot, eaten with bread cubes.

Raclette

Melted cheese scraped onto potatoes, pickles, and onions – a cozy Alpine dish.

Rösti

A crispy potato pancake, originally a Swiss-German breakfast, now a national favorite.

Swiss Chocolate

Famous worldwide, Swiss chocolate brands like Lindt and Toblerone are global icons.

Traditional Swiss Dress

Traditional Swiss clothing varies by region:

  • Women: Dresses with aprons, embroidered blouses, lace, and corset-style bodices. Often decorated with floral patterns.
  • Men: Embroidered shirts, breeches, suspenders, and felt hats. In alpine regions, lederhosen and wool jackets are common.

These costumes are worn during festivals, folk dances, and national events such as the Swiss National Day (August 1st).
